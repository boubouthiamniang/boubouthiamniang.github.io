# About

My name is Boubou Thiam Niang. I'm a PhD student at the Decision and Information for Production Systems (DISP) laboratory and at Berger-Levrault. My thesis focuses on interoperability solutions to support frequent changes in tightly coupled systems and transform them into scalable and maintainable systems-of-systems (SoS). To achieve this, I work with several techniques, such as software product line engineering, Delta-oriented programming (DOP), and model-driven engineering (MDE). 

Indeed, inter-application interoperability solutions are often developed on an ad hoc, manual basis. Apart from the sheer number of links leading to the spaghetti effect, developing these interoperability solutions is time-consuming in development and maintenance. Each change in the specification of a customer or internal business application often requires adaptation of the other business applications.

For interoperability that promotes decoupling, my research focuses on event-driven architectures. The interaction between legacy applications is modeled by identifying and modeling enterprise integration patterns. From this model, we can automatically generate the modules required for business applications.
